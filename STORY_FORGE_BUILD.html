⭐ What we're working toward (the unconventional edge)

We are NOT building another cloud model-runner. The whole bet is to beat the vendors by doing it 100% local with new pathways nobody else is using:
  • LANG
    Our own language — the .sf DSL. Write a film in plain text (scenes, motion, voices, music); it compiles to the render plan. Nobody ships a film-director language; everyone ships node graphs.
  • QUANT
    New quantization pathways — 1-step distillation. We forged our own self-consistency objective and got Wan down to a single step (LPIPS 0.052, beats the wall). That's the frontier — distill-on-Apple-Silicon is unpublished territory.
  • VOICE
    Cloned, consistent character voices (ChatterBox), fully local. Each character = one frozen reference clip = same voice every time. No cloud TTS.
  • SPEED
    Stacked local speedups — LTX (5.6×) + 1-step distill + smart routing, all on the M5 (128GB). Goal: a 4-min film in minutes, not 5 hours.

🧪 What we tried — and what we'll NEVER do again (and why)

NEVER: repaint mouths (Wav2Lip / box / video-retalking).

These are trained on HUMAN mouths. Forced onto a stylized bear they paint a blurry human mouth on a muzzle = looks horrible, "a still with a mouth that doesn't match." Rejected on sight. Dead forever.

NEVER: narrator talking over characters whose mouths move.

A single narrator voice over characters visibly "talking" reads as nonsense. Every character gets their OWN voice speaking their OWN lines.

WON'T WORK: timing voice to the animation's ambient mouth motion.

Wan adds RANDOM idle jaw-flap to make a still feel alive — it's not forming words, so there's nothing to sync to. No timing trick can match arbitrary audio to random motion.

DEAD END: Metal flash-attention kernel.

2–4× slower than Apple's MPS SDPA; the "12× win" was a measurement bug. Documented, disabled, don't reopen.

SKIP on M5: Q4 quantization.

Was only a trick to fit the mini's 64GB. On the 128GB M5 we render full fp16. Don't quantize here.
