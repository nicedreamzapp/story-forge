🔒 Locked method — talking characters (the hardest case)

The proven recipe for when characters speak on screen. Other video (narrated, ambient, promo) skips most of this.
  • 1
    One scene at a time → you OK it → LOCK the .mp4 → never touch it again.
  • 2
    Voice only over the untouched clip — never repaint mouths (Wav2Lip/face-models banned).
  • 3
    Match voice to the mouth by eye — density-matched, one talker per beat; never voice a closed mouth or leave a moving one silent.
  • 4
    Keep each line inside its clip; silencedetect confirms no bleed across cuts.
  • 5
    Agent self-verifies (frames + audio) before you ever see it. You are never the QC tester.
  • 6
    Assemble dialogue-only scenes → concat → ONE continuous song over the whole piece.

Doug = your cloned voice · Hank = ChatterBox built-in seed 44 (never cloned) · 3 spares. Full doc: SCENE_BUILDING_METHOD.md

⚠️ The rule — log everything, never repeat it

Every build and every mistake is written down here (this page + SESSION_HANDOFF.md + SCENE_BUILDING_METHOD.md) so we never re-learn it the hard way. The standing never-agains:
  • ×
    Never repaint mouths (human face on a stylized muzzle = rejected on sight).
  • ×
    Never let voice carry across a scene cut (wrong character seems to talk) — verify with silencedetect.
  • ×
    Never trust the ChatterBox voice meter (degenerate); voice identity = you confirm once, then locked.
  • ×
    Never build all scenes at once, and never ship without self-QC'ing frames + audio first.
  • ×
    Dead ends, disabled, don't reopen: Metal flash-attn kernel (slower than MPS), Q4 on M5, wan_metal_fused node (crashed renders).
